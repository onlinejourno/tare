Findings at a glance

RTB Cascade

Each bar represents one request to an ad-tech intermediary, timed from navigation start. This is the ~100ms auction that broadcasts reader identity to every buyer simultaneously.

Google Ecosystem Attribution

Percentage of all network activity attributable to Google infrastructure — the same company whose Core Web Vitals algorithm penalises the resulting page slowness.

Consent Timing Audit

Consent Dark Patterns

Design tricks that manipulate readers toward accepting tracking. A Computers & Security study found 90% of popular websites use them — pre-ticked boxes, buried reject options, accept/decline visual asymmetry.

Ad Blocker Wall Test

Page loaded a second time with common ad domains blocked (simulating uBlock Origin). Does the site penalise readers who protect their own privacy?

Access & Paywall

Is this journalism freely available to all readers, or gated behind money or data? Democratic journalism assumes universal access — paywalls and registration walls create different information environments for different economic classes.

Participation & Editorial Transparency

Does the publication provide infrastructure for readers to engage, correct, and hold the journalism accountable? RSS feeds, bylines, corrections policies, and editorial standards statements are measurable proxies for institutional openness.

AI Editorial Infrastructure

To what degree are editorial decisions — what readers see, which headline they read, when they hit a paywall — made by algorithmic systems rather than editorial judgment? These systems are invisible to readers but shape the information environment profoundly.

Data Flow Audit

When you load this page, who learns what about you? This audit maps the actual flow of reader data — cookies set, identity sync requests, outbound data signals, and which organisations receive your information.
